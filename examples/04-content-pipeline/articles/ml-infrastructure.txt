Building Reliable ML Infrastructure

Modern ML systems fail in ways that differ from traditional software. A model that
produces confident but wrong predictions is harder to detect than a service returning
a 500. Reliability engineering for ML requires new primitives: data validation at
ingestion, model performance monitoring, and — critically — the ability to replay
failed batch jobs from their last checkpoint.

The most common failure mode in production ML is silent: the pipeline completes,
the predictions are stored, and nobody notices the input data was malformed until
a downstream report shows anomalous numbers three weeks later.

Layered validation — at ingestion, at feature extraction, and at prediction time —
addresses this. Each layer is a checkpoint. When one fails, you resume from that
layer, not from data collection.
