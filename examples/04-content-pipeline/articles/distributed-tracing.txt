Distributed Tracing at Scale

Distributed tracing solves a specific problem: when a request passes through
fifteen services and something is slow, which one is responsible? Without tracing,
the answer is "run the same request again and add logging everywhere". With tracing,
you have a timeline.

The operational cost of tracing is not instrumentation — it is sampling. Tracing
every request at high throughput is expensive. Head-based sampling (decide at the
first service) is simple but misses rare tail-latency events. Tail-based sampling
(decide after the trace is complete) is accurate but requires buffering full traces
before making a decision.

Most teams start with head-based sampling at 1–5% and add tail-based sampling for
error traces only. This covers 95% of debugging needs at a fraction of the cost.
